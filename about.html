About this journal

Our Codebase Has Ghosts

Follow our adventures in building Skill Weaver, an adaptive assessment passion project. We, the builders, write about the architecture decisions, the testing stories, and the research behind the adaptive algorithm. Meet the recurring characters who live in our codebase and make the project come to life.

The cast

Vampire

Impeccably dressed and relentlessly opinionated our vampire is convinced that every interface deserves to look spectacular. He lives for elegant typography and symmetric layouts that nobody asked for, but everyone secretly appreciates. He can detect misalignment from across the room and has absolutely moved a button three pixels because “the vibes were off.” He’s definitely a monster, but have you ever seen an unattractive vampire? Exactly.

Ghost

She is the caretaker of Haunted Bicycle’s infrastructure. Equal parts engineer, and librarian, she spends her days shushing noisy logs, reviving Docker containers, and wandering the halls tightening whatever mysterious bolt is about to fall next. She keeps the load-bearing configuration files upright, and production from collapsing under its own supernatural weight. Around here, every deployment follows the same sacred ritual: push to GitHub, then call on the Ghost to make it happen.

Spider

It is endlessly curious and obsessively meticulous. While everyone else is fixing bugs or debating button placement, the Spider quietly searches for patterns, and spins the web that powers adaptive assessment. Every so often it decides the infrastructure would be more interesting if it were woven into the web as well, leaving the Ghost to gently untangle several innocent configuration files from a masterpiece nobody asked for. Spider calls this “adding context", rest of the team calls it "feature creep."

What we cover

  • Architecture: decisions we made about how Skill Weaver is put together, and the tradeoffs behind them.
  • Database: schemas, queries, and the data shapes that quietly steer the product.
  • Adaptive assessment: the research behind item response theory, adaptive testing, and preserving instructor agency in assessment design.
  • Deployment: the unglamorous, load-bearing work of keeping everything running.
  • Craft: reflections on the lore of the cast and how they relate to us.
  • Pedagogy: teaching, learning design, and how assessment should serve instructors and students alike.
  • Design: color theory, typography and the interaction design choices that carry Skill Weaver.
